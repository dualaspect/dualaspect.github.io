After my life-long friend and lover died, at the age of twenty one, having
decided that a death of quick and terrible violence was preferable to a life of
slow and tortuous agony, I have failed to achieve a total state of peace. To
escape from this pain I have strived to rise like foam in the troubled waves
that roar beneath a storm; foam which even prefers the storm and wish that it
may be tempestuous, not to reach greater heights but rather to increase their
distance from the depths. For my age, I have achieved a certain degree of
success in mundane affairs, or at least what I and a small circle of equally
ignorant people might call success: a position in a prestigious university,
a publication in a \textit{Nature} journal, the respect of my pears and
